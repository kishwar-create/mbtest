What tools does a locksmith use?

Unlocking the Mystery: What Tools Does a Locksmith Use?

Have you ever found yourself staring in awe as a locksmith quickly and effortlessly picks a lock? Maybe you've even wondered what tools they use to work their magic. Well, wonder no more! In this article, we're going to unlock the mystery and reveal exactly what tools a professional locksmith uses to get you back into your home or car.

From traditional lock picks to more advanced electronic tools, locksmiths have a variety of tools at their disposal to help them do their job. You may be surprised to learn just how intricate and specialized some of these tools are, and how much skill and experience it takes to use them effectively.

Whether you're a curious home owner looking to expand your knowledge or you're currently locked out and want to know what to expect, read on to discover the fascinating world of locksmith tools!

Basic Locksmith Tools

A locksmith's job involves repairing, installing, and unlocking various types of locks. To perform these tasks, they must have the right set of tools. Here are some of the basic locksmith tools:

Key Extractor

One of the most common issues that locksmiths deal with is a broken key. In such a situation, a key extractor becomes handy. It is a thin metal tool designed to remove the broken end of the key from the lock. The locksmith inserts the key extractor into the keyway, rotates it, and pulls the broken key out.

Tension Wrench

A tension wrench is an essential lock picking tool. A locksmith uses it to apply pressure to the lock's inner cylinder while picking it with a pick. By using a tension wrench, the locksmith can create a false set in the lock and release the cylinder's pins.

Lock Picks

Lock picks come in different shapes and sizes and allow a locksmith to manipulate the lock's pins to unlock it. Lock picks can be of two main types: hook picks and rake picks. Hook picks are used to lift each pin, while rake picks help the locksmith pick multiple pins at once. Locksmiths often carry a variety of lock picks for different locks.

Drill

When a lock cannot be picked, drilling is often the last resort. A locksmith drills a hole through the lock's housing, allowing access to the lock's internal mechanism. They can then manipulate the locking mechanism and unlock the door. However, drilling damages the lock, making it unusable.

Bolt Cutters

Bolt cutters are large cutting tools used to cut padlocks, chains, and other locking mechanisms that are difficult to pick. While a locksmith tries to avoid damaging locks, bolt cutters are a necessary tool in some situations.

These are some of the basic tools that every locksmith must-have. Professional locksmiths often carry a broader range of gear for specific locks, including key cutting machines, rekeying kits, and plug spinners. With these tools, a locksmith can provide top-of-the-line lock and security services.

Advanced Locksmith Tools

Locksmiths are skilled professionals that can unlock and repair locks with ease. They have a wide range of tools that they use to get the job done. In addition to the standard tools that we discussed in the previous section, locksmiths also use advanced tools that can help them tackle complex lock-related problems. Here are some of the advanced tools commonly used by locksmiths:

Key Extractors: These tools are used to extract broken keys from locks. They have a long, thin design that can reach deep inside the lock to pull out the broken key without damaging the lock itself.

Tubular Lock Picks: Tubular locks are commonly found in vending machines and other coin-operated devices. They have a unique locking mechanism that requires a specialized tool to unlock. Tubular lock picks are designed specifically for this purpose.

Electric Picks: These are powered tools that use electricity to move the lock pins up and down, simulating the action of a key. They are useful for opening highly secure locks that cannot be picked manually.

Lock Decoders: These tools are used to decipher the code of a lock, making it possible to create a duplicate key without the original key. They are commonly used by locksmiths to create keys for high-security locks without the need for a key copy.

Key Programmers: These tools are used to program electronic keys for cars and other vehicles. They can create a new key or reprogram an existing one in case of a lost or stolen key.

In conclusion, locksmiths use a range of tools to unlock, repair, and install locks. Advanced tools help them to tackle complex lock-related problems quickly and efficiently. If you need the services of a locksmith, make sure to hire a professional with the right tools and expertise to ensure a safe and secure job.

What Are Automotive Locksmith Tools?

When it comes to vehicles, a locksmith needs to be equipped with a specialized set of tools. Automotive locksmith tools are designed to help locksmiths handle different types of car locks efficiently. These tools allow them to open locks without causing any damage to a vehicle's door or ignition system. One of the essential tools for every automotive locksmith is a slim jim. It is a flexible metal strip with a hook on one end that can be inserted between a car's window and its rubber seal. This tool is used to manipulate the locks from inside the car door, giving access to the vehicle's interior. Another important tool for automotive locksmiths is a key extractor. It is used in situations where a key has broken off inside the ignition or door lock. The extractor is used to remove the key without causing damage to the lock cylinder. Locksmiths also use a transponder key programmer. It is a device that helps create new transponder keys and program them to work with a specific car. Without this tool, it would be challenging to create new car keys, and the process would take much longer. Finally, locksmiths use a wedge to help create a gap between a car's door and frame. This wedge is necessary to insert other tools and manipulate the lock mechanism. It prevents damage to a car's paint or door rubber seals. In conclusion, automotive locksmith tools play a vital role in helping locksmiths gain access to vehicles without causing damage. With the right tools and training, locksmiths can provide their customers with high-quality service when it comes to car lock-related issues.

Electronic Locksmith Tools

Locksmiths now rely on electronic tools to aid in various tasks. These tools are used for high-security locks, electric locks, and safes. Electronic tools allow a locksmith to provide fast and efficient service while ensuring optimum security.

One of the most popular electronic tools used by locksmiths is the electric pick gun. It is used to manipulate the pins in a lock easily. The tool vibrates the pins up and down, allowing the locksmith to open the lock without using a key. The electric pick gun is especially useful when dealing with lockouts or lost keys.

Another electronic tool commonly used by locksmiths is the key decoder. The key decoder is a small device that can determine the exact cuts needed to create a replica of a key. The locksmith can then create a new key without the original, saving time and money for both the locksmith and the client.

A third electronic tool that locksmiths use is the drill rig. The drill rig is used to create a hole in a safe or a lock without damaging the surrounding area. It is often used when the locksmith needs to gain access to a safe or lock but lacks the key or combination code.

Lastly, locksmiths also use electronic key cutters, which use computerized technology to cut keys. These machines can duplicate keys and create new ones based on codes matching the original key or lock. Electronic key cutters are faster and more precise than manual key cutters, ensuring that clients receive accurate and reliable keys.

In conclusion, electronic tools have revolutionized the locksmith industry. These tools are essential for locksmiths to provide efficient and reliable service to their clients while also ensuring that the client's property remains secure.

Specialized Locksmith Tools

Aside from the basic tools, locksmiths also use specialized tools to handle various lock types and situations. These tools may include:

1. Pick Guns – a tool used to open a lock by triggering the pins inside the lock to jump and unlock the cylinder.

2. Plug Spinners – a tool used to turn the lock plug instead of using the key. This tool is handy when the key is lost, or the lock is damaged.

3. Tubular Lock Picks – designed specifically to pick tubular locks, they work by lining up the pins to unlock the lock cylinder.

4. Wafer Picks – designed to pick wafer locks, which are commonly found in car doors and ignition systems.

5. Jiggler Keys – used to manipulate the pins in a lock to unlock it, especially for car locks.

6. Key Extractors – a tool used to remove broken keys from locks.

7. Locksmith Scopes – used to view the inside of a lock cylinder and examine the different components of the lock.

8. Broken Key Extractors – specifically designed to remove broken keys from the lock cylinder.

9. Lock Picking Sets – a comprehensive set of tools used to pick various types of locks to gain access.

These specialized locksmith tools are invaluable in handling different lock situations. Locksmiths must be familiar with the various tools and techniques to efficiently and effectively pick a lock, repair it or change it. Locksmiths invest heavily in buying these tools that help them perform their job with skill and precision.

The Key to Success

In conclusion, locksmiths play a crucial role in our lives, providing us with the security and peace of mind we need when it comes to protecting our belongings and loved ones. Although some may think that the profession is all about breaking locks, in reality, locksmiths are highly skilled professionals who use a wide range of tools to solve complex security problems. From simple lockouts to sophisticated security systems, they are experts in their craft and work tirelessly to keep us safe.

So next time you need a locksmith, don't hesitate to call on their expertise. You'll be impressed by their knowledge, dedication, and cutting-edge tools. And who knows, you might even learn a thing or two about the fascinating world of locks and keys!
